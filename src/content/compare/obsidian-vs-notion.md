## Verdict

Choose Obsidian if your first priority is owning your files, writing in Markdown, and building a long-term archive that survives tool changes.

Choose Notion if your first priority is fast collaboration, shared docs, and lightweight project databases that the whole team can update in one place.

## Why this comparison matters

People often compare Obsidian and Notion as if they do the same job. They overlap on note-taking and documentation, but they optimize for different constraints:

- Obsidian optimizes for local ownership, offline access, and Markdown portability.
- Notion optimizes for team collaboration, linked pages, and structured workspace convenience.

## Offpedia recommendation

- Durable solo writing: Obsidian + GitHub
- Shared team docs: Notion or Docusaurus, depending on whether you need docs engineering or fast workspace editing
- Personal wiki publishing: Obsidian + Quartz
